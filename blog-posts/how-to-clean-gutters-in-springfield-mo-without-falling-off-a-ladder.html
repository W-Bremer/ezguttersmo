How to Clean Gutters in Springfield, MO (Without Falling Off a Ladder)

January 24, 2025

Introduction
Gutter cleaning isn’t glamorous, but in Springfield, MO, it’s essential. With our heavy oak and pine tree coverage, clogged gutters can lead to water damage, pest infestations, and even foundation issues. Whether you’re a DIY enthusiast or prefer to leave it to the pros, this guide will help you tackle gutter cleaning safely and efficiently.

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

  • Sturdy Ladder: Opt for a 4-legged ladder with stabilizers (avoid leaning on gutters!).
  • Gloves & Gutter Scoop: Protect hands from sharp debris and sludge.
  • Garden Hose with Spray Nozzle: For flushing downspouts.
  • Safety Goggles: Shield eyes from falling debris.

Local Tip: Springfield’s McCoy’s Building Supply stocks gutter scoops and heavy-duty gloves perfect for Ozarks’ pine needles.

Step 2: Safety First

  • Check Weather: Avoid cleaning during rain or high winds.
  • Ladder Setup: Place the ladder on level ground, and have a spotter hold it steady.
  • Power Line Awareness: Stay 10+ feet away from overhead lines.

Step 3: Scoop Debris

  • Start near the downspout and work outward.
  • Use the gutter scoop to remove leaves, pine needles, and sludge.
  • Springfield Hack: Line a bucket with a trash bag to collect debris—no messy cleanup!

Step 4: Flush the System

  • Use a garden hose to flush remaining dirt toward the downspout.
  • If water pools, check for clogs in the downspout. A plumber’s snake can dislodge stubborn blockages.

Step 5: Inspect for Damage

  • Look for cracks, rust, or sagging sections.
  • Pro Tip: Snap photos of problem areas to share with EZ Gutters for a free repair estimate.

When to Call EZ Gutters

  • Steep Roofs: Springfield’s historic homes often have steep pitches.
  • Recurring Clogs: If you’re cleaning monthly, gutter guards are a smarter investment.
  • Time Constraints: Let us handle the dirty work while you enjoy your weekend.

Conclusion
